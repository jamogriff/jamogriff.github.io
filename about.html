Jamison Griffith is a full-stack developer based out of Arvada Colorado. In 2015 he graduated with a Bachelor of Science degree in Integrative Biology at CU Denver. After working a couple of years as a medial writer and a clinical microbiology lab tech he began exploring ways to pivot to programming.

In 2018, he accepted a role as Senior Prototyping Specialist for the interdisciplinary Inworks lab at CU Denver. He worked with physicians to 3D-print novel anatomical models, provided prototyping services for entrepreneurs during the 10.10.10 startup program and developed a web app to allow staff to manage the lab access and credentials of more than 600 users.

All said and done, he taught more than 180 Inworks workshops in 3D-printing, laser-cutting, programming, electronics, CNC milling, and 3D CAD design to undergraduate/graduate students and professionals.

He enrolled in the Backend Engineering program at the Turing School of Software and Design in 2021. Turing gave him the opportunity to learn the principles of OOP, TDD and MVC architecture and apply them on a half dozen projects for more than 40 hours a week for ~7 months.

Later that year he began working at Zing Studios as a full-stack developer. Since then he has designed and built a data warehouse, implemented dozens of APIs, launched custom eCommerce sites, and built state-driven frontend and admin applications for a SAAS product.

He strives to write SOLID and robust code that meet his clients' needs and deadlines. He's just as comfortable implementing interfaces on the backend as creating modular styles on the frontend. He can churn out a lot of code using just a Terminal, git and Docker.
